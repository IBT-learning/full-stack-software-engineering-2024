The Crying Stone of Illesi, Kakamega

Crying has always been thought to be a phenomenon exclusive to human beings. This is not the case for inanimate objects except for one special stone that can only be found in Kenya, specifically in Kakamega County, The Crying Stone of Illesi

Why does the stone cry?

Legend has it that it was once a happily married man. He had no troubles, and, therefore, no reason to cry. One day, the man woke up to discover that his beloved wife had left him for another man. His heart was broken, and he wept inconsolably. Ever since that tragic day, the man has been waiting for his wife to return, but as she never has. So, he stands alone on the side of the road, weeping, with no one to comfort him. Eventually, he turned into a rock, but his tears continued to flow, earning him the name, The Crying Stone of Illesi.

More about the Crying Stone

To most locals however, the stone is known as Ikhongo Murwi.And to a naturally sceptical scentists, The Crying Stone is just a large boulder, balanced on a column of rock, with water flowing from a groove at its centre. However to the Luhya Community who have lived around the stone for hundred of years, Ikhongo Murwi has a much deeper significance. To them it is a sacred scriptural and cultural site.

  1. It is a signifier of rainy season
  2. It is a source of "magical protection"
  3. It is a boundary signifier between the Luhya and Nandi Communities
  4. The caves beneath it are used for healing rituals

In recent years the tears of the crying stone of Illesi have dried up and there are different speculations as to why that might be the case. Here are some reasons why:

  • Because the heartbroken man has finally moved past his sadness toward his wife's departure
  • The Presence of a eucalyptus tree nearby sucking up most of the groundwater
